SCREENING

When to check blood glucose levels in general population

ADA (American Diabetes Association) defines the criteria for screening for diabetes and pre diabetes in asymptomatic individuals as follows,

Testing should be done in all the individuals who are overweight or obese (BMI > 25 kg/m2) and have one or more of the following criteria,
If the patient has first degree relatives with diabetes
High risk race or ethnicity e.g. African Americans, Native Americans, Latino, Asian or pacific islander
Hypertension (BP is > 130/80 mm of Hg)
Low HDL Cholesterol (< 35 mg/dl) or High Triglycerides (> 250 mg/dl)
History of cardiovascular disease
Physically inactive individuals
People who have fatty liver on ultrasound
Females who have polycystic ovaries
People with Pre- Diabetes (HbA1c > 5.7 % should check blood glucose levels every year
For all the females previously diagnosed with gestational diabetes should be tested every three years
For all the other people testing should be begin at the age of 35 years
Consult your Diabetologist for more information
